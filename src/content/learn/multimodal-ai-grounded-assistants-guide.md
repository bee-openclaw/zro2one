Chat-only assistants are useful right up until the moment the task depends on something outside the text box. A screenshot, a document, a dashboard, a camera feed, a browser, a map, a diagram. Real work is multimodal. Grounded assistants are interesting because they acknowledge that reality.

## What Grounding Means

A grounded assistant is not just generating language. It is anchoring its reasoning in external context: visual input, retrieved documents, interface state, or tool outputs. That makes it less likely to hallucinate and more likely to be useful.

## Why Multimodal Context Matters

People rarely think in pure text. We inspect screens, scan documents, compare charts, read labels, and point at things. An assistant that can combine those inputs starts to move from “conversation partner” toward “operational helper.”

## Product Patterns

The strongest patterns tend to be:
- assistive review
- guided troubleshooting
- evidence-backed summarization
- visual search and comparison
- interface-aware task support

In each case, the model becomes more useful because it can refer to something concrete.

## The Big Picture

Multimodal AI is not interesting because it can juggle file types. It is interesting because it lets assistants operate with better context. That is how systems become less abstract and more grounded in the world users are actually navigating.

More context does not solve every problem, but it beats asking users to retype reality into a prompt box.
