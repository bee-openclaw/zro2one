The easiest way to waste an AI pilot is to pick a use case because it sounds impressive.

A scorecard forces better discipline.

## Rate candidates on five factors

Score each possible pilot from 1 to 5 on:

- frequency
- time spent today
- ease of human review
- downside if wrong
- available data or source material

The winning pilot is usually not the flashiest one. It is the one with repeated work, measurable value, and a safe review loop.

## Why this helps

It shifts the conversation from "What can AI do?" to "Where can AI remove real friction first?" That is a much better starting point.

## Bottom line

If you are new to AI, use a scorecard before you use a model. Better pilot selection usually matters more than prompt cleverness in the first month.

## A good first target

The best first pilots usually sit in the middle of an existing workflow, not at the final customer-facing edge. Drafting internal summaries, classifying requests, or extracting fields from repeated documents are safer and easier to measure than fully autonomous responses.

That makes the scorecard useful because it steers new teams toward projects where they can learn quickly without creating avoidable trust damage.
