Many organizations install cameras and assume coverage is solved. It usually is not. The real question is whether the camera network actually sees the areas, events, and object paths that matter.

Coverage analysis is where video AI becomes operationally useful.

## What coverage analysis means

A coverage system tries to answer questions like:

- where are the blind spots?
- which zones have overlapping visibility?
- where does lighting or occlusion make detection unreliable?
- which routes are poorly observed during normal activity?

This is not just about field of view on paper. It is about effective visibility in real conditions.

## Inputs that matter

Useful analysis combines:

- camera placement and calibration
- scene geometry
- historical motion patterns
- detection confidence by zone
- time-of-day lighting changes

A hallway that looks covered at noon may be effectively blind at night.

## Why teams use it

- security operations
- warehouse safety
- retail loss prevention
- facility design reviews
- industrial monitoring

The value is not only catching incidents. It is reducing the number of places incidents can hide.

## Key takeaway

Camera coverage analysis is one of the more grounded uses of video AI because it focuses on system design, not just event detection. Sometimes the best model improvement is moving the camera.
