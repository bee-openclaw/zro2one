Store audits are a classic multimodal problem. You need to compare what is visible in the store with what the business expected to be there.

That means combining images with structured context: planograms, pricing tables, promotion schedules, and store metadata.

## Why multimodal helps

An image alone can show an empty space. It cannot tell you whether that space is a stockout, a reset in progress, or a display that was supposed to change this morning. Business context turns a visual signal into an operational decision.

## Bottom line

Multimodal AI becomes useful in retail when it closes the gap between visual evidence and the next store action. That is the point of the system, not image recognition by itself.

## The operational test

Ask whether the surfaced issue leads to a useful task for staff. If the answer is no, the audit is still incomplete. Detection without action is just another dashboard.

That is why strong store-audit systems connect directly to tasking, escalation, or manager review rather than stopping at a confidence score.

## Where teams fail first

Most failures come from weak alignment between image capture and business state. If the timestamp is wrong, the planogram is outdated, or the store metadata is incomplete, even a strong vision model will surface noisy findings.

That is why the project is not only a model problem. It is also a systems-integration problem.
